How It Works

We redirect existing economic flows (like referral commissions and aligned services) into scholarships and microgrants for community health projects.

1

Partners

Relocation services, financial advisors, staffing agencies, and other aligned partners participate through service agreements or referral revenue.

2

Funding Pool

Proceeds flow into the REACH Foundation to fund scholarships and microgrants focused on SDOH outcomes.

3

Scholars

Providers and trainees apply for support that protects time and resources to execute high-impact projects.

4

Impact

We track measurable outcomes—like housing stability, food security, and preventive care access—and publish transparent reports.

For Scholars

Who Can Apply

Medical students, residents, fellows, nurses, PAs, allied health professionals, and practicing providers with a community impact plan.

What We Fund

  • Protected time for SDOH projects
  • Community partnerships & materials
  • Evaluation & outcome measurement

Selection Signals

  • Clear problem & local need
  • Feasible plan with partners
  • Measurable outcomes & sustainability
